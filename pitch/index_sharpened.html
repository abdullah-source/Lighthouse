The whole loop

How it fits together.

The shift

Buying decisions are moving inside the model.

A growing share of "what should I buy" now starts with an AI assistant, not a search box. There is a Search Console for Google. There is no console for the answer engines.

ChatGPT Claude Perplexity Gemini Google AI Overviews
The problem

Brands are flying blind in the place buyers now ask.

Do I show up

No reliable read on whether the AI recommends you for real buyer questions.

Who shows up instead

No view of which competitors win the answer, on which question, on which engine.

And why

The hardest one. Nobody tells you the actual reason the AI picks them and skips you.

Why this vs that

The market is a variable. Execution is the constant.

We are not betting the company on one fixed market staying still. Engines, surfaces, and buyers will shift. What compounds is depth of execution in one category: a denser, truer dataset than a horizontal tool can clone with a feature.

So we go deep in one consumer category first, and let the measurement quality become the moat.

The foundation: the measurement layer

Not a vibe check. A benchmark.

Individualised input

Buyer prompts seeded from the brand's own voice of customer: reviews, tickets, search logs. Not 20 generic guesses.

Frozen panels

Prompt sets locked into versions, so trends are real and a result can be reproduced and compared over time.

Real engines, retrieval on

Probe the surfaces the way buyers actually hit them, and capture the sources the answer cited.

Generic everywhere is a commodity. Deep and grounded in one vertical is defensible.

The differentiator

The "why" is off-site citation provenance.

A leaderboard says "you are at 0%." That is not actionable. We capture the exact sources the engine cited, and show the gap:

"Perplexity recommends the competitor for this query. It cited RunRepeat, Reddit, and Wirecutter. You appear in none of them. Here is the list of sources to go earn."

That turns a number into a diagnosis, and hands the team the precise places the AI actually trusts. It is valuable on day one, with no dependency on shipping a change fast.

The moat

Measurement and action are commoditizing. Proof is open.

Anyone can show a dashboard. Anyone can generate content. Almost nobody can prove a change caused a measurable lift in AI recommendations.
