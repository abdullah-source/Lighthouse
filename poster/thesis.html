1The problem

Buyers now ask AI what to buy. It names a few brands. If you are not one of them, you are invisible, and you never see the customer you lost.

2How it works
  • Ask GPT-5, Claude, and Perplexity real buyer questions; capture who wins and the pages they cite.
  • Index the answers with embeddings into pgvector.
  • Reconstruct retrieval: rank candidate pages by cosine to the query, then compare to what the engine cited.
  • Validate against the real citations, build the fix, and re-measure.
3Why we win

Everyone measures the answer. We model the retrieval behind it, and the labeled data compounds with every audit, a moat a downstream tool cannot collect.

4The proof

Then the loop closes: ship the change, let the crawlers re-read the page, and re-run the audit. That before-and-after is the proof, not a projection.

We generate publish-ready content and schema.org markup from the exact language the AI rewards, then drop it on the page. On the next crawl the engines read it, and we re-measure across all three to show the real movement, not a projection. It runs on about a cent per audit: a control system for retrieval, not a telescope on the answer. And every audit adds to the labeled dataset, so the reconstruction sharpens with each brand we measure.
