## 1. Where we started

- Product: measure whether mid-market consumer / DTC brands appear in LLM
  recommendations, who appears instead, and what to change. "Console for the
  LLM era."
- Reality at session start: a working **v0** engine (queries to probe to parse
  to normalize to report) plus a v1 web app shell. Prior session had already
  killed "we act, they don't" and "neutrality is the moat," and landed on
  causal proof + one-vertical depth as the differentiator.

## 2. The interrogation (the professor's questions)

We pulled the product apart honestly, grounded in the actual code:

- **Input / queries.** Queries are generated by one Claude call (`queries.py`)
  from just brand + category. Conclusion: not random, but generic and
  non-reproducible. Surface-level.
- **Neutrality.** The probe is neutral (the brand name is hidden from the
  answering model), but the query *set* is LLM-invented and changes every run,
  so it is not a stable benchmark.
- **The action wedge, honestly.** `action.py` / `/api/recommendations/generate`
  sends only `{brand_id, competitor}`, grabs ONE arbitrary sample query, and
  returns a generic FAQ. It is not tied to the queries where the brand loses
  and has no idea why the competitor wins. Verdict: **demo-ware, "just words."**
- **The model choice.** We probe GPT-5 and Claude as **no-retrieval chat**,
  which is both unrealistic (real users get retrieval answers) and
  un-actionable (a content change cannot move a weights-only model fast).
- **Can a brand even change fast enough?** Two clocks: publishing is fast
  (minutes on Shopify/Webflow); propagation into AI answers is days for
  retrieval engines, months/never for plain chat. On-site content is the floor;
  off-site / third-party sources are the bigger, slower lever.
- **Why no citations?** Because we were not probing a retrieval engine and the
  parser discarded any URLs. The "why" did not exist in our data.

## 3. The strategic decisions made this session

1. **Narrow to fast-shipping customers.** Stop aiming the core loop at slow
   consumer brands that cannot publish on our timeline. Favor DTC-on-Shopify /
   the segment that ships same-day (agency / B2B SaaS held as alternatives).
2. **The differentiated "why" is OFF-SITE citation provenance.** Capture the
   sources a retrieval engine cites and show "competitor wins here, cited these
   sources, you are in none." Valuable day one, no dependency on the customer
   acting fast.
3. **Causal proof becomes a free byproduct, not a slow gate.** Auto-snapshot
   before/after on a frozen panel. The fast product ships value daily; the
   proof and the moat accrue silently underneath. Do not delete it (deleting it
   makes us a Profound + AirOps clone); do not let it slow the product.
4. **Fast-track product = RAG-grounded measurement + a collaborative content
   workflow.** We suggest changes from results, the customer adds their own
   context and ideas, we co-generate the final content, multi-use output.
5. **Action, re-scoped, = on-site crawler / agent readiness.** Find and fix the
   real blocks that stop AI crawlers (robots.txt blocking GPTBot/PerplexityBot,
   JS-only rendering, status/redirect issues, missing schema, anti-bot walls,
   thin content), at catalog scale. Tier 2 (later) = server-log / CDN bot
   analytics, like Profound's Agent Analytics.
6. **RAG is now in scope** (reversing the earlier "premature" call) for one
   specific job: ingest the company's first-party context (reviews, tickets,
   search logs, docs) to (a) individualise the query panel and (b) ground the
   content. Start simple (retrieve-and-stuff), add a vector store (pgvector)
   when the corpus is large.

## 4. The pivot we REJECTED (and why it matters)

A parallel Gemini thread pushed a full rebrand to "Lighthouse" and a
repositioning around an **entity-graph + `llms.txt`** thesis.

- **Adopted kernel:** off-site sources drive recommendations; a "provenance /
  where did it learn that" view is strong; the Brooks/On case is a great
  entity-resolution story.
- **Rejected:** building the product on `llms.txt` (an unproven standard;
  Google has said it does not use it) and "one-click Wikidata sync" (really PR /
  editorial, not an API). And rejected whipsawing the positioning + regenerating
  a deck hours before a pitch.
- **Rule of record:** absorb the off-site insight into the "why"; do not bet the
  company on `llms.txt`; do not whipsaw on a confident AI narrative.

## 5. The sharpened idea (one paragraph)

A deep measurement layer for one DTC vertical: individualised buyer queries
seeded from the brand's own customer language, frozen into versioned panels, run
across real engines with retrieval on. The differentiated output is the off-site
"why" (the sources the AI cites and where you are absent). Action is making the
brand's own property readable and preferred by AI crawlers, plus a collaborative
content workflow. Proof of causal lift accrues quietly in the background and
becomes the moat over time. The whole thing is hard to copy because it is
accumulated data + method in one vertical, not code.

## 6. The workflow (the chart)

```
 INGEST            MEASURE             DIAGNOSE             ACT               PROVE
 brand + VoC   →  scheduled runs   →  off-site citation →  on-site crawler →  hold frozen
 + competitors    across real          provenance: which   readiness fixes    panel,
 → individualised  engines,            sources the AI       + collaborative    re-measure,
 frozen query      retrieval ON,        cited, where you    content workflow   attribute the
 panel             + cited sources      are absent          (we suggest, you   lift
                                        (the "why")          add context, we
                                                             co-generate)
                          │                                                         │
                          └──────────────────  ASK  ◄───────────────────────────────┘
        (agent / search bar over the brand's own data: explore + simulate, instant)
```

Two loops: the **fast loop** (explore queries + simulate a change in the search
bar, instant, in the marketing team's hands) feeds the **slow loop** (real
engines re-crawl, re-measure the frozen panel, prove the lift). The search bar
is the daily surface; the frozen panel + real-engine re-measure is the proof.

## 8. The forward build plan

1. **(DONE) Perplexity + citations** — the off-site why is real.
2. **Make RAG real — context ingestion.** Customer brings in first-party context;
   store + retrieve it; use it to generate the individualised, frozen query
   panel AND to ground content. Start retrieve-and-stuff; add pgvector later.
   This is the biggest anti-vibecode piece (kills surface-level queries).
3. **Collaborative content workflow.** Results + provenance + customer context →
   suggested change → customer adds ideas → co-generate final → multi-use export
   (product page, FAQ, comparison, blog, social). Approval gated.
4. **Auto-snapshot before/after** — the free proof byproduct, on the existing
   `experiments` scaffold.

Optional near-term sharpening: turn provenance from "top cited domains" into
"sources where you are absent" (requires fetching each source).

## 9. V2 prospects

- **RAG at scale:** embeddings + pgvector on Postgres (Supabase) for large
  first-party corpora and an "ask anything across your category" surface. Skip
  dedicated vector DBs.
- **Agent / crawler analytics (Tier 2 action):** server-log / CDN integration to
  observe AI bots actually crawling, and stopping. The literal "the bot stopped
  watching" signal, plus stronger proof that a re-crawl happened.
- **Reputation / hallucination monitor:** track what the AI *says* about the
  brand (false claims, stale facts, sentiment) with alerts. Different buyer
  (brand / comms), same pipeline. Strong alternative wedge.
- **Pre-publish recommendability simulator:** estimate how engines would treat a
  draft before publishing. The causal engine as a testing product; natural home
  for the Ask search bar.
- **MCP server:** let a marketing team's own agents (Claude Desktop, Cursor)
  query their visibility data natively, instead of only a browser dashboard.
- **Causal-proof case studies:** the accrued before/after lifts become the
  marketing and the moat once volume exists.
- **Engine breadth:** add ChatGPT-with-search (reuse OpenAI key) and Gemini
  grounding so measurement and provenance span all the surfaces buyers use.

## 10. Competitive positioning (of record)

- **Profound** = measurement leader (demand-grounded prompt volumes, real
  engines, citations, agent analytics; enterprise, expensive).
- **AirOps** = content/action factory (bulk workflows, CMS push; growth teams).
- **Us** = one DTC vertical, deep: off-site citation provenance as the "why",
  on-site crawler readiness + collaborative content as the action, and causal
  proof accruing as the moat. We win on depth + proof, not on breadth.

## 11. Honest cautions carried forward

- Crawlability is necessary, not sufficient: fixing blocks does not guarantee a
  recommendation; off-site presence sets the ceiling.
- On-site content moves retrieval engines, not weights-only chat, in the short
  run. Demonstrability lives on retrieval surfaces.
- Provenance citations explain (correlation); proof requires the controlled
  before/after. Do not conflate them in a pitch.
- Cost discipline: individualised + frozen panels run on a schedule keep spend
  bounded and predictable per brand; parsing stays on the cheap model.

## 12. Open decisions

1. Which differentiated layer leads commercially: provenance + content now, with
   proof accruing (current lean), vs. reputation monitor, vs. action-led.
2. Keep consumer/DTC narrowed to fast shippers, or move the buyer (agency /
   B2B SaaS) where shipping is the customer's job.
3. Provenance depth: ship "sources where you are absent" before Step 2, or go
   straight to RAG context ingestion.
